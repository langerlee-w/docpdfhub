Method 1 — Merge PDFs online (no upload)

DocPDFHub runs fully in your browser. That means your files never leave your device and merging is instant. Here’s how:

  1. Open the Merge PDF tool.
  2. Click Select PDFs (or drag & drop) to add two or more PDF files.
  3. Drag the list to reorder files as you like.
  4. (Optional) Keep metadata checked if you want to preserve title/author info.
  5. Click Add PDFs to Merge and wait a moment.
  6. Download merged.pdf — you’re done!

Tips for a perfect merge

  • File order matters: put the cover or the first chapter at the top.
  • Mixed orientations: portrait/landscape pages merge correctly as-is.
  • Big files? After merging, run the result through Compress PDF to shrink size while keeping quality.
  • Remove a few pages first: Use Split PDF to extract only the pages you need, then merge.
  • Images needed? Convert any PDF to pictures with PDF to JPG before or after merging.

Alternatives on Mac/Windows/Mobile

macOS (Preview)

  1. Open the first PDF in Preview, enable the Thumbnails sidebar.
  2. Drag the second PDF from Finder into the sidebar to insert it.
  3. Reorder thumbnails, then File → Export as PDF.

Windows (Microsoft Print to PDF)

  1. Open PDFs and print to Microsoft Print to PDF selecting pages in order.
  2. It’s clunky and re-rasterizes; for best quality use our online merge above.

Mobile

On iOS/Android, open docpdfhub.com/tools/pdf-merge.html in your browser and add files from Files/Drive. Everything runs locally in the tab.

FAQs

Is it really private?

Yes. All processing happens in your browser via WebAssembly/JS; nothing is uploaded to a server.

What’s the max file size?

Practically it’s limited by your device memory and browser tab. For very large documents, merge in batches then merge the results.

Will bookmarks/outlines be kept?

Basic metadata can be preserved. Complex outlines aren’t copied by most client-side libraries; after merging, you can add bookmarks in a desktop editor if needed.

What Is PDF Compression? Explained for Non-Engineers

Lossy vs. lossless, DPI, and safe presets you can use today.

PDF compression reduces file size so your documents are easier to share and store. The key is choosing the right balance between visual quality and bytes. Here’s a plain-English overview.

Lossless vs. Lossy

  • Lossless: Optimizes structure without removing information (e.g., object streams, metadata cleanup).
  • Lossy: Mostly affects images (JPEG quality). Smaller files, potential detail loss at aggressive settings.

What Settings Matter?

  • DPI (downsampling): 110–150 for screens; 150–200 for light print; 300 for high-quality print.
  • JPEG Quality: 0.6–0.85 is a good range for readability and size.
  • Metadata: Remove non-essential data to save space and protect privacy.

Recommended Presets

  • Light: 150 DPI, high JPEG quality (≈0.9), keep metadata → best visual quality.
  • Balanced: 110 DPI, medium JPEG (≈0.75), strip metadata → great for email.
  • Strong: 90 DPI, lower JPEG (≈0.6), strip metadata → smallest size for quick sharing.

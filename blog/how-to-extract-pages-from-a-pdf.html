How to Extract Pages from a PDF (3 Simple Ways)

Split by ranges, pick single pages, or export images — no upload, fully private.

Need to extract a few pages from a long PDF? Here are three quick, private methods you can use in your browser with DocPDFHub—no sign-up, no upload.

Method 1 — Split by Page Ranges

  1. Open the Split PDF tool.
  2. Add your PDF and choose “By page ranges”.
  3. Enter ranges like 1-3,6,9-10 and click Split.
  4. Download the resulting PDFs (zipped if multiple files).

Method 2 — Export Each Page to Its Own PDF

  1. Go to Split PDF.
  2. Select “Each page into a file”.
  3. Process and download — every page becomes a separate PDF.

Method 3 — Save Pages as Images (JPEG)

  1. Open PDF to JPG.
  2. Optionally set DPI and quality, pick your page range.
  3. Convert and download the ZIP of page images.

Tips for Accuracy & Quality

  • Use page ranges for precision (supporting non-consecutive pages).
  • For sharing, images (JPG) are often more convenient.
  • Everything runs locally in your browser for maximum privacy.
